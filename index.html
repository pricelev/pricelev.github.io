Guardian Classes

Titan

In Destiny the titan guardian class is similar to a tank in other games. It's build feature a bulkier physique due to the large shoulders of the character. To fill out this tank role, the titan has unique abilities in its kit such as walls to protect itself and his allies. The titan also wants to be physical so they are guedared with ways of getting into and out of melee range.

Hunter

In Destny the hunter guardian class is similar to that of a rogue. They like to take battles at a distance due to their ket. They also provide lots of mobility that other classes do not have. These can be seen in their jump mechanics and their ability to dodge. Their playstle tends to corrolate with more aggressive players due to this mobility. Most of their supers are offensive in nature.

Warlock

In Destiny the warlock guardian class fits into multiple columns of playstyle. They have the ability to be physical with life regen abilites. They also tend to be the most supportive class in the game. THis is due to their use of rifts to either heal or amplify damage depending on the situation. The warlock tend to me the more versitle guardian in Destiny because of various play styles.

Game Types

Gambit

Gambit is a multiplayer game type introduced in Destiny 2: Forsaken. It is run by The Drifter, a rogue Guardian. It is a hybrid PVP/PVE mode that features two teams of four as they compete across three rounds.

Iron Banner

Iron banner is a pinacle PVP mode that comes three or four times a season. It's main gametype is control with a variant on normal rules. The Iron Banner is ran by Lord Salidin and can be accessed by all players in the game.

Strike

A Strike is a cooperative activity available in Destiny. A Strike is a structured, progressive, cooperative adventure "ideal for pick-up groups or friends looking for a quick battle together." Strikes are replayable, designed for three-person Fireteams.
